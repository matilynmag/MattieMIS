A Guide to Formula 1

What is Formula 1?

Formula One is the highest class of international racing for single seat, open wheel formula race cars. F1 was established as an accredited motorsport in the mid 1900s.

Quick Info

  • There are 20 total F1 teams, each with two drivers
  • Each team purchases or builds engines, and the rest of the design in up to the team, within the current sport regualtions.
  • There are currently 23 races in the F1 calendar. The races are internationally spread and cover 6/7 continents.
  • There are 3 standard tyre types; softs are the fastest but quickest to degrade; mediums have moderate degredation and speed; hards are slowest, but most durable
  • Wet weather doesn't discourage racing! There are two additional wet weather tyres.
  • The top 10 finishers for each race earn a certain amount of points, with the fastest lap earning a point as well
  • There are two seperate competitions that take place. One is the World Driver Champitonship, which is individual competition between drivers. The other is the Constructor's Championship which factors each teams' two drivers' points into a total.
  • Lewis Hamilton and Michael Schumacher are currently tied for the record of most World Driver Championships
